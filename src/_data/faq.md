# Organifanten FAQ

_Common questions from people encountering Organifanten for the first time._

---

## What is Organifanten?

**1. What is Organifanten, and who is it for?**

Organifanten is a sensemaking tool for people working in complex organizations who repeatedly encounter unresolved tensions — things that feel off, unclear, or somehow wrong, without being able to name them immediately. It is aimed at consultants, leaders, agile coaches, and anyone who observes organizational dynamics and wants a structured space to reflect on them.

---

**2. What does the name "Organifanten" mean?**

The name is a portmanteau of _organization_ and _elephants_ (Elefanten in German). It refers to the "elephants in the room" — tensions and dysfunctions that everyone sees but nobody speaks about. Organifanten gives you a place to consciously capture and work with exactly those things.

---

**3. How is this different from a task manager like Jira or Trello?**

A task manager handles tasks and decisions. Organifanten works one layer beneath that: with _tensions_ — raw observations that are not yet clearly defined problems. You are not formulating solutions; you are documenting what you perceive. The tool helps you recognize patterns over time before moving to action.

---

**4. Is Organifanten a project management tool?**

No. There are no sprints, milestones, or resource planning. Organifanten is a reflection tool for observing organizational dynamics, not a tool for executing projects.

---

## Core Concepts

**5. What is a "tension"?**

A tension is a raw observation: something feels wrong, unclear, contradictory, or blocked — but you don't yet know exactly why or what to do about it. Tensions are not immediately framed as problems. You log them narratively and observe how your understanding evolves over time.

---

**6. What is a "Tension Moment"?**

A Tension Moment is an entry in the history of a tension. There are three kinds:

- **Insight** — a realization or a recognized pattern
- **Intervention** — a deliberate action taken in response to the tension
- **Effect** — an observable consequence of an intervention

Together, these moments form a timeline that shows how your understanding of a tension develops.

---

**7. What is a "Local Cloud"?**

A Local Cloud is your private cognitive workspace focused on a specific system — for example, your team or a process. All tensions, moments, and interventions you capture live inside a cloud. It is visible only to you. You can have up to five clouds, one per system.

---

**8. What is an "intervention"?**

An intervention is a deliberate action you plan or carry out in response to a tension. Interventions have three states:

- **Draft** — an intention with no date
- **Planned** — a commitment with a future date
- **Done** — an action with a past or today's date

The state is computed automatically from the date.

---

**9. What is a "system" in Organifanten?**

A system is a unit of your organizational context — a team, a process, an external stakeholder. Each cloud is focused on exactly one system. Systems can be arranged in hierarchies (e.g., a team as part of a department).

---

**10. What is the difference between a tension and an intervention?**

A tension is an observation — something you perceive. An intervention is an action — something you do. Interventions are always linked to a tension and emerge gradually as part of the sensemaking process.

---

## Privacy & Data

**11. Can my team see my tensions?**

No. Every cloud and everything inside it is completely private. Other users in the same account cannot see your tensions, moments, or interventions. This is a deliberate design decision: sensemaking requires a safe space for unfinished thoughts.

---

**12. Can multiple people work on the same tensions?**

Not yet. Organifanten is currently a single-user tool. Cloud sharing between users within the same account is planned as a future feature but is not yet available.

---

**13. What happens to my data when I delete my account?**

Deleting your account permanently removes all associated data — systems, clouds, tensions, and moments. This action cannot be undone.

---

## Getting Started

**14. How do I get started?**

Register with your name, email, and the name of your organization. During registration, you can immediately name a system and describe a first tension. Afterwards, you land directly in your first cloud and can start working right away.

---

**15. Do I need to have a tension in mind to register?**

No. Capturing a first tension during onboarding is optional. You can add tensions later at any time.

---

**16. How many clouds, tensions, and systems can I create?**

- **Clouds:** up to 5 (one per system)
- **Systems:** unlimited
- **Tensions per cloud:** unlimited
- **Moments per tension:** unlimited

---

**17. How do I prioritize my tensions?**

In the tensions backlog, you can reorder tensions by drag-and-drop. The order reflects your personal priority within that cloud.

---

## Modelling

**18. What is the system modelling for?**

System modelling gives your tensions a context. You describe how your organization is structured — units, roles, hierarchies. This helps you locate tensions where they arise and recognize patterns at the system level.

---

**19. What are roles and unit types?**

- **Roles** are account-level functional definitions (e.g., "Engineer", "Team Lead"). They can be assigned to positions within organizational units.
- **Unit types** are labels for organizational units (e.g., "Team", "Department"). They help categorize units consistently across your account.

---

## Who Is It For?

**20. Who is Organifanten _not_ for?**

Organifanten is not the right fit if your primary need is managing tasks, projects, or resources. It is not a reporting tool and not a replacement for ticketing systems. It is for people who work through reflection and deliberate engagement with organizational tensions — not for those who primarily want to track and execute work items.
